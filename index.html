About

I started in tech and never looked back. In high school, I wanted to level up from console gaming to a PC, but prebuilt systems were out of my budget—so I built my own. That decision sparked my passion for technology and problem-solving.

I quickly became the go-to “IT” person for friends and family, which led to my first role as a Help Desk Technician right out of high school. My curiosity pushed me beyond basic support, and that’s when I discovered network engineering. With mentorship from a network administrator early in my career, I earned my Network+ and CCNA.

Today, I sharpen my skills by building real-world labs that mirror enterprise environments—firewalls, DMZs, segmentation, secure service publishing, and hybrid cloud security. I also enjoy mentoring others and helping friends break into IT.
