About Me

I started in tech and never looked back.

In high school, I spent a lot of time gaming on console and wanted to level up to a gaming PC. On a tight budget and unable to afford prebuilt systems, I stepped outside my comfort zone and built my own. That decision sparked my passion for technology and problem-solving.

I quickly became the go-to “IT” person for friends and family, which led to me starting my career as a Help Desk Technician right out of high school. My natural curiosity pushed me to go deeper than basic support, and that’s when I discovered network engineering — and I was hooked.

With mentorship from a network administrator early in my career, I pursued and earned my Network+ and CCNA certifications. Since then, I’ve focused on building real-world lab environments that mirror enterprise networks — working with firewalls, segmentation, secure service publishing, and hybrid cloud security.

Today, I continue sharpening my skills through hands-on labs and studying advanced networking and cloud technologies. I also enjoy mentoring others — helping friends with no direction break into IT and build careers of their own.
